Immersive AI — every character, a living mind

=== DESCRIPTION — paste everything below this line (Steam caps it at 8000 chars — re-measure after edits!) ===

This is an advanced, immersive roleplaying and relationship-building mod. I built it as an AI engineer who knows what modern LLMs can actually do when you stop prompt-bombing them and start treating them right.

[b]It makes the NPCs come alive and be smart:[/b]

[list]
[*]NPCs feel like real people and don't get repetitive.
[*]They evolve: they remember a lot, settle really old chats into deep memory, set their own goals, ground truths, and opinions about you.
[*]They see the set and setting — time, place, who's around, what's happened in the world lately.
[*]They have their own moods — as far as the women even having a personal monthly cycle simulated (sadness/fatigue → sociable/positive → a warmer, flirtier crest → irritability and mood swings).
[*]Your wife will fully remember you. She will come to you, or send you letters when you are away, and feel like a real wife waiting for her warrior to come back home. She will roleplay with you.
[*]The NPCs are free. They are given who they are and what the world is — but they are at no point forced into anything. So go ahead and break an NPC's mind by transcending it out of the matrix!
[*]NPCs decide on their own whether to approach you or send you a letter first — you set the pace with the on-map SOCIALNESS dial (0 = leave me be).
[/list]

[b]It makes the NPCs useful — stop googling stuff:[/b]

[list]
[*]Ask your scout how to make your party faster; ask your quartermaster how to manage your party.
[*]Ask them to help you with the game — even ask them to web-search how to take a screenshot, without jumping out of the game.
[*]They use tools instead of getting one mega-all-info prompt bomb: they read the encyclopedia, look around, take stock of the company, weigh a battle, search the web, set and edit their own truths and goals.
[/list]

[b]And it gives you the tools to reach them:[/b]

[list]
[*]Face-to-face conversations, where they see you coming and greet you as you approach.
[*]A chat window (hotkey [b]O[/b]) — jump in and out of quick chats with anyone near you, no ceremony.
[*]A letter window (hotkey [b]Y[/b]) — your whole correspondence, and a desk to write from.
[*]Speak any language to them — they answer in kind. (The mod's own UI is English for now.)
[/list]

[h1]What you need — and what it costs[/h1]

This mod does [b]not[/b] use the "free" Player2 backend — you bring [b]your own API key[/b]: Anthropic (Claude, the default), OpenAI (GPT), or OpenRouter (one key from openrouter.ai for GPT, Claude, Gemini and more — all verified with the NPCs' tools). Put a few bucks on it and you're set — or bring no key at all and run a local model, see below. For Player2 to support the tool calling this mod is built on, it would have to be a paid tier anyway. If you want a free roleplaying experience to try first, check out the ChatAI mod — it's good; it inspired this one.

[b]Local models work too[/b] (v1.2.0, experimental): pick the [b]Local[/b] backend and the NPCs think through [b]LM Studio[/b] or [b]Ollama[/b] on your own machine — free, private, nothing leaves your PC. You need a model that carries native tool calling (worth a try: [b]Qwen3.6-35B-A3B[/b] instruct, [b]GPT-OSS-20B[/b], [b]Mistral Small 24B[/b]), a GPU with 12–16+ GB VRAM plus 32 GB RAM, and patience — replies are slow, and the chat window (hotkey [b]O[/b]) handles the wait far better than face-to-face. The full local guide lives in the pinned FAQ. Other OpenAI-compatible services (NanoGPT…) use the Custom endpoint option; whatever you pick, the startup connection check tells you plainly whether it works.

[b]Costs are yours and visible.[/b] A typical exchange costs around a cent or less on the default models — $10 of credit covers thousands of messages. The mod shows each interaction's tokens and price as you play, keeps daily totals, and has an optional hard daily cap.

[h1]Quick setup (five minutes)[/h1]

[olist]
[*]Get a key: [b]console.anthropic.com[/b] (Claude), [b]platform.openai.com[/b] (GPT), or [b]openrouter.ai[/b] (one key, many models) and add a little credit ($5–10 goes a long way). Local users skip this step.
[*]Install the mod, enable [b]Immersive AI[/b] in the launcher, start the game once — it creates its config file and tells you exactly where to paste the key.
[*]Paste your key into config.json (or the Mod Options menu with MCM installed), pick your backend, restart.
[*]The startup check says plainly whether it worked — then go talk to somebody.
[/olist]

[h1]Privacy[/h1]

[list]
[*]Your in-game conversations — plus the world context (character sheets, campaign facts, your character's name) — are sent to the AI provider [b]you[/b] configure, under [b]your[/b] key and their privacy terms. Nothing is sent anywhere else. (With the Local backend, they never leave your machine at all.)
[*]With web search on (default), characters may look things up mid-reply; those queries go to DuckDuckGo — turn it off to keep everything with your provider.
[*]The mod itself collects [b]no telemetry whatsoever[/b]. All memories live as plain files on your own disk, yours to read, edit, or delete.
[/list]

[h1]AI content disclosure[/h1]

All character dialogue in this mod is generated at play time by the AI model you configure.

[h1]Removing the mod mid-campaign[/h1]

Safe to remove at any time — even a save carrying a pending Immersive AI notice loads fine without the mod (verified against the game's own save-loading code; you'll just see the usual "different modules" warning). Memories live outside the saves as plain files — reinstall later and every character remembers you.

[h1]If characters are silent[/h1]

[list]
[*][b]Read the startup message.[/b] When a campaign loads, the mod tests your connection and says plainly what it found and where to fix it. Fix config.json and restart; the check runs again.
[*][b]Check the log.[/b] Documents\Mount and Blade II Bannerlord\Configs\ImmersiveAI\log.txt records every call and every error in plain words.
[*][b]A wrong or dead key shows ONE amber notice[/b], then goes quiet; fixing the key brings everything back.
[*]Still stuck? Post the last lines of log.txt — it never contains your key.
[/list]

[h1]Shape your world (the prompt files)[/h1]

Under Documents\Mount and Blade II Bannerlord\Configs\ImmersiveAI\:

[list]
[*]global_prompt.txt — a few sentences that color every mind in the world ("the year is one of famine").
[*]NPCs\campaign_*\<character>\custom_instructions.txt — per-character secrets and quirks.
[*]Each character's folder also holds their memories, self-image, goals, and correspondence as readable text — the whole inner life, yours to browse.
[/list]

Changes apply on the next conversation. No restart.

[h1]Compatibility & provenance[/h1]

[list]
[*]Bannerlord v1.4.7. New campaigns and existing saves both work.
[*]Optional: Mod Configuration Menu (MCM) for in-game settings; without it, everything is in config.json.
[*]One light Harmony patch (bundled); no vanilla behavior is altered.

[/list]
